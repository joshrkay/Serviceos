# ServiceOS — 90-Second Video Demo Script & Storyboard

_For use by video production team. Narrated demo of core product flows._

---

## Production Overview

**Runtime:** 90 seconds  
**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover + B-roll cutaways  
**Tone:** Direct, honest, working-class. Not startup-y. Mike should watch this and nod.  
**Music:** Subtle acoustic guitar loop under narration — something that sounds like it belongs in a truck at 6am, not a Silicon Valley pitch deck.  
**Color grade:** Warm, slightly desaturated. Real-world feel.

---

## Scene-by-Scene Storyboard

### Scene 1 — 0:00–0:07 (7s) | Problem Hook
**Visual:** Dark kitchen, 5:45am. Phone face-down on counter. Screen lights up — 6 missed calls. Mike's face, stressed. Static shot.  
**Narration:** "It's 6am. Mike Rivera runs a two-truck HVAC shop in Phoenix."  
**Production note:** No music yet — just ambient kitchen sounds. Cold open.

---

### Scene 2 — 0:07–0:15 (8s) | The Problem
**Visual:** Montage: Mike typing texts one-handed at the kitchen table. Kids visible in background. Gas station burrito in truck. Missed call notification.  
**Narration:** "Without ServiceOS, that means 45 minutes of apology texts, estimates from memory, and one job lost to a competitor who picked up."  
**Production note:** Music fades in quietly mid-scene.

---

### Scene 3 — 0:15–0:26 (11s) | Morning SMS Digest
**Visual:** Phone screen — crisp SMS from "M&R Mechanical." Show the message:
```
Good morning, Mike. 6 calls overnight.
✅ 4 booked (Tue & Wed)  
🤔 1 needs you — Mrs. Alvarez, no AC, 2 small kids.
   Tap YES to patch her through.
✅ 1 out-of-area, declined politely.
```
Finger taps "YES." Quick cut to phone ringing.  
**Narration:** "With ServiceOS — it means one text. The AI answered every call overnight. Booked four jobs. And flagged the one that needs Mike."  
**UI highlight:** Amber highlight ring pulses around the YES button when tapped.

---

### Scene 4 — 0:26–0:38 (12s) | Estimate Approval
**Visual:** App screen — Estimates queue. Three cards. Each shows: customer name, job description, total, confidence indicator.  
Card 1: Green confidence → Mike taps "Approve ✓"  
Card 2: Green → tap "Approve ✓"  
Card 3: Amber warning flag — "⚠ I used last year's rate ($122/hr vs your recent $145/hr). Want me to update?"  
Mike taps the number field, dictates "make it 145." Updates. Taps "Approve ✓".  
**Narration:** "Three estimates are waiting — built from call recordings and his price book. The third has a flag. The AI used last year's labor rate and knew it. Mike fixes it in 10 seconds."  
**Production note:** Show the confidence flag prominently — this is a differentiator.

---

### Scene 5 — 0:38–0:48 (10s) | Invoice + Payment
**Visual:** Invoice detail page — line items, total, "Send" button. Tap send → SMS to customer → customer phone receives it → payment link in SMS → customer taps link → Stripe payment page → "Payment confirmed" screen.  
**Narration:** "When jobs are done, invoices go out automatically. Payment links arrive by text. Most pay within the hour."  
**Production note:** Speed up the payment flow to show it as frictionless. Don't linger on Stripe UI.

---

### Scene 6 — 0:48–1:02 (14s) | AI Assistant
**Visual:** Assistant chat interface. Mike types (or voice inputs): "What did we quote the Patel job?"  
AI response in 4 seconds: "You quoted $425 — diagnostic + capacitor. Carlos added a contactor ($225). Invoice is now $650. Want me to text Carlos to confirm the scope change with the customer before invoicing?"  
Mike taps "Yes."  
**Narration:** "When Carlos books extra work on a job, Mike doesn't need to chase the paperwork. He asks. Gets an answer in 4 seconds. Problem solved before it becomes one."  
**UI highlight:** Response time counter visible: "4s"

---

### Scene 7 — 1:02–1:15 (13s) | End-of-Day Digest
**Visual:** Phone → SMS digest arriving at 7:03pm. Show the full message:
```
M&R Mechanical — End of day

✅ 4 jobs done ($3,840 invoiced)
💰 $2,100 already paid via link
📋 3 follow-ups sent on unpaid invoices
   → 1 paid. 1 promised Friday. 1 silent.
   Want me to call the silent one?

Tomorrow: 6 jobs confirmed.
Nothing else needs your approval.

What I wasn't sure about today:
- Patel part # (flagged, you confirmed)
- Mrs. Alvarez urgency (I offered 6pm, she took it)
```
Mike reads it. Closes phone. Kids in background.  
**Narration:** "Every night at 7pm — what got done, what's owed, and what the AI wasn't sure about. It's the only AI back office that tells you what it got wrong."  
**Production note:** Linger 2 extra seconds on "What I wasn't sure about today." This is the trust differentiator.

---

### Scene 8 — 1:15–1:25 (10s) | Payoff
**Visual:** Mike at dinner table with his kids. Real meal. Laughing. Phone is face-down. Wide shot, warm lighting.  
Then cut to: Mike in bed at 9:45pm reading a book. Wife sleeping. Peace.  
**Narration:** "Mike reads it at dinner. He's in bed by 9:45. ServiceOS — you learned the trade. We'll run the business."  
**Music:** Swell slightly, then fade.

---

### Scene 9 — 1:25–1:30 (5s) | End Card
**Visual:** Logo on dark background. URL: serviceos.com. Tagline: "Start your free 14-day trial."  
**Narration:** "Start your free trial at serviceos.com."  
**Production note:** Clean, no clutter. Phone number optional if available.

## Post-Production Notes

- **Subtitles:** Full CC for social media versions (85% of mobile video plays muted)
- **Cut-downs needed:** 15s (Instagram), 30s (pre-roll), 60s (YouTube)
- **The 15-second version:** Scenes 3 + 7 only — the morning text and the dinner payoff
- **Social thumbnail:** Mike's face + the "6 missed calls" screen + "There's a better way" text overlay
- **No stock footage of laptops or servers** — this product is used on a phone, not a computer. The entire demo should feel like it happens in a truck.
