# 06 — Track 6: Voice Corpus, Training & Evaluation Pipeline

Date: 2026-07-18 · Read-only discovery · Evidence verified against HEAD `a9d06aa`
See `discovery/00-cartography.md` for orientation.

## Summary

The eval infrastructure here is genuinely two-tier: the **voice-quality harness** (`packages/api/src/ai/voice-quality/` + five CI workflows) is unusually mature and real — PR-blocking Layer-1 cassette replay with an enforced launch gate, spend-capped Layer-2 release gating with 3-run voting and five graders, a daily real Twilio call smoke against staging, and workflow-pinning tests — while the **corpus/data pipeline** (`data/corpus/`, `scripts/data-pipeline/`, `scripts/eval/`) is in a silently broken state. Two independent corpus-building passes (June 8's 3,034-row pass and a later 3,220-row bilingual pass) were merged at PR #609 into the *same file* with incompatible schemas: `data/corpus/utterances.jsonl` now holds 4,854 mixed-schema rows that crash the Python eval harness (`KeyError: 'id'`) and fail both competing validators. Nothing noticed because **zero corpus gates run in CI** — `test:corpus-schema`, `test:dedup`, `test:pii-leakage`, and `eval:*` appear nowhere in `.github/`. `CORPUS_MANIFEST.md`'s counts (3,617) are wrong; the real committed labeled-row count is **6,651**. The taxonomy has triple-drifted: production `SUPPORTED_INTENTS` has 60 intents, `data/behaviors.yaml` has 41, `data/corpus/behaviors.yaml` has 35 — including 9 corpus labels the production classifier can never emit, which puts a structural ~9–10pp error floor under the weekly live 92% intent gate. The Reddit pipeline (`serviceos_training/`) is competently built (real PII scrubber ported from prod, 27 tests) but is a complete island whose output feeds nothing. The operator's "~305 labeled utterances" is actually 305 *transcripts* (`generate-transcripts.ts` `TARGET = 305`); the labeled-utterance reality is 20× larger than claimed, and the "agent-behavior model" is a YAML taxonomy document, not a model and not enforced at runtime.

## Findings

**T6-F01 | Mixed-schema corpus file breaks the entire offline eval/validation stack | Fix | Critical**
Evidence: `data/corpus/utterances.jsonl` contains two key-shapes — 1,820 rows `{id,text,intent,lang,code_switch,source,reviewed_by_human}` and 3,034 rows `{utterance,intent,slots,source,confidence,reviewed_by_human}` (verified by row-level parse). `scripts/eval/run_eval.py:57` (`split_of(r["id"])`) raises `KeyError` on id-less rows → `pnpm eval:full/edge-cases/negatives/spanish` all crash. `validate-corpus.ts:39` fails 3,034 rows ("missing id/text/intent"); `validate-utterances.ts` `RowSchema` (requires `utterance`,`slots`,`confidence`) fails the other 1,820; both dedup scripts key on the field the other generation lacks. Both generations declare the same output path (`corpus-lib.ts:12`, `lib.ts:9`). Merged at `5042bd3` (PR #609, 2026-06-21). Only `packages/voice-eval/run-intent-eval.ts:56-64` survives (normalizes `r.text ?? r.utterance`).
What & Why: The repo's flagship "labeled corpus + eval harness" is un-runnable as committed; regression detection via `previous_metrics()` is moot. Effort: **M** (choose one canonical schema, migrate 3,034 legacy rows — they carry richer `slots` labels worth keeping — regenerate, re-run all gates). Confidence: **High** (static, deterministic).

**T6-F02 | No corpus/eval gate runs in CI — the breakage was invisible by design | Gap | Critical**
Evidence: `grep -r "corpus:|test:dedup|pii-leakage|run_eval|eval:full|corpus-schema" .github/` → zero matches. `pr-checks.yml` runs typecheck/lint/unit/integration/voice-quality only. The PII "HARD STOP" (`pii-leakage.ts:2`), schema floors, dedup, and the entire Python eval are local-only npm scripts.
What & Why: Every promise in `data/corpus/README.md` ("HARD STOP on any PII", frozen-split regression detection) is aspirational — nothing enforces it on merge, which is exactly how T6-F01 shipped. Effort: **S** (add one PR job: `test:corpus-schema && test:dedup && test:pii-leakage`, plus `eval:full` once F01 is fixed). Confidence: **High**.

**T6-F03 | Triple taxonomy drift: prod 60 ≠ behaviors 41 ≠ corpus 35; 9 corpus labels are unclassifiable → weekly live 92% gate has a structural error floor | Fix | Critical**
Evidence: `SUPPORTED_INTENTS` in `packages/api/src/ai/orchestration/intent-classifier.ts` = **60** intents. `data/behaviors.yaml` = 41 (its own header: "derived directly from … SUPPORTED_INTENTS"); `validate-behaviors.ts:6-7` asserts "exactly that set (no missing, no extra)" → now fails. `data/corpus/behaviors.yaml` = 35, of which 9 don't exist in prod: `agent_handoff_request, callback, dnc_optout, draft_invoice, greeting, hours_location, payment_dispute, pricing_question, service_availability`; conversely **18 prod intents have zero corpus coverage** (`approve_proposal, reject_proposal, edit_proposal, lookup_day_overview, lookup_digest, lookup_job_profit, lookup_pending_items, batch_invoice, negotiation, update_job, …`). `live-support.ts:230-232` compares `gold` to `res.intentType` with **no label mapping**. ~468 of 1,820 new-schema EN rows carry impossible golds; on the mixed 4,854-row split that's a ~9.6% accuracy ceiling deficit vs the 0.92 `--gate` in `voice-eval-live.yml`.
What & Why: The Monday live gate is either permanently red for structural (not model) reasons, or red-blindness has already set in; and the intents most in need of eval (proposal approve/reject/edit — the FSM-level actions) are exactly the untested ones. Effort: **M** (label-map or relabel the 9; author corpus for the 18; re-sync both behaviors files). Confidence: **High** on the drift; **Medium** on the exact pp floor.

**T6-F04 | `run_eval.py` grades a shadow rule classifier hand-fit to its own synthetic corpus — accuracy saturated at 1.0 | Gap (eval theater) | High**
Evidence: `eval-results/2026-06-09/metrics.json`: `intent accuracy 1.0, en 1.0, es 1.0, gap 0.0 (n=645)`. `classifier.py:1-8` is "ordered, weighted lexical patterns," authored in the same pass as the templated corpus it's scored on. `run_eval.py:280-283` regression check compares against a metric that cannot go up and will only move if rules/corpus are edited together.
What & Why: As "AI regression detection" this is theater — it never touches the production model (that's `voice-eval --live`'s job) and its thresholds are trivially exceeded by construction. Its honest value is corpus QA/consistency, and even that is currently broken (T6-F01). Effort: **S** (reframe as corpus-lint; drop the 92% framing) or **L** (point it at prod classifier). Confidence: **High**.

**T6-F05 | Spanish is un-gated everywhere that matters | Gap | High**
Evidence: `run-intent-eval.ts:47` loads only `data/corpus/utterances.jsonl` (EN + legacy EN) — the 1,400-row ES corpus never reaches the live weekly gate. The only working ES coverage is the Layer-1 `11-spanish` bucket: **4 scripts** (`corpus/scripts/11-spanish/`, threshold 0.9 at `graders/report.ts:121`). `eval:spanish` (the 5pp-gap check, `run_eval.py:44,277`) is a saturated-rule-classifier check and currently crashes (T6-F01).
What & Why: A bilingual product whose ES eval reality is 4 cassette-replayed scripts; a Spanish-only prompt regression in the real classifier would ship undetected until the Layer-2 release gate at best. Effort: **M** (load `utterances_es.jsonl` in the live eval with its own gate; grow the Spanish bucket). Confidence: **High**.

**T6-F14 | Where the stack IS real: layered gates that can genuinely catch a shipped AI regression | Positive | —**

The candid in-file postmortems (`voice-quality-nightly.yml`; `run-slot-eval.ts` refusing to rig `service_type`) indicate real engineering honesty. The gaps above are concentrated in the *corpus* layer, not the *harness* layer.
