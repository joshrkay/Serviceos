# Keyword / Intent Map — Rivet ServiceOS

Compiled 2026-07-10 via WebSearch (see methodology note in `competitor-facts.md` — WebFetch was non-functional this session; page-level facts below come from WebSearch's own fetch+synthesis pass, with the source URLs it returned). "Difficulty notes" name the actual pages/domains that surfaced in results, not a numeric score — treat as qualitative SERP-composition intel.

**Cross-cutting SERP finding:** almost every cluster below is dominated by two kinds of pages, neither of which is Jobber/Housecall Pro/ServiceTitan's own site: (1) programmatic-SEO comparison/"pricing breakdown" mills (myquoteiq.com, fieldcamp.ai, buyersprint.com, procured.us, projul.com, contractoropsguide.com, getonecrew.com, tekpon.com) publishing near-identical "2026 pricing guide" content for every FSM tool, and (2) a crowded tier of point-solution AI-answering-service vendors (Marlie, Dialzara, Rosie AI, AgentZap, RingReady, NextPhone, Smith.ai, Whippy, Goodcall, evs7, Ciela, upfirst.ai, SkipCalls, VoiceCharm) that only do call-answering, not the back office. This is a structural opening: nobody ranking today combines "answers the phone" with "runs the back office" as one product — that's the wedge to hammer in title tags and H1s.

## Cluster 1 — "Jobber alternative(s)"
- **Representative queries:** jobber alternative, jobber alternatives 2026, best jobber alternatives for small contractors, jobber alternative for plumbers, cheaper alternative to jobber, jobber vs [X]
- **Intent:** comparison / transactional (bottom-funnel — already Jobber-aware, shopping)
- **Target page:** `/vs-jobber` (primary), secondary listicle-style article for the "alternatives" plural phrasing
- **Difficulty notes:** SERP is a wall of affiliate/programmatic comparison content — Foreman, getonecrew.com ("15 Best Jobber Alternatives"), SimplyWise, scanmanifold.com, thedigitalprojectmanager.com, buildbite.com, contractoropsguide.com, nuacom.com, myquoteiq.com, fieldcamp.ai all rank with nearly interchangeable "best alternatives" listicles. None of them lead with "the AI answers your phone and runs your back office" — they compare feature checklists and price tables. Jobber itself does not appear to rank for its own "alternative" query (unsurprising) but does run offense on adjacent terms (see Cluster 2 — Jobber has a `/academy/housecall-pro-competitors/` page attacking Housecall Pro).
- **Angle we win:** Every alternative list frames the choice as "which paperwork tool is prettiest." None mention that Jobber's new AI Receptionist is call/text-only, Jobber-ecosystem-only, and billed per-conversation past 30/mo. Our angle: "Jobber gives you better paperwork. Rivet does the paperwork" — position as a category difference (does-the-work vs. helps-you-do-the-work), not a feature-parity fight.

## Cluster 2 — "Housecall Pro alternative"
- **Representative queries:** housecall pro alternative, housecall pro alternative for small hvac/plumbing, best housecall pro alternatives 2026, companies like housecall pro
- **Intent:** comparison / transactional
- **Target page:** `/vs-housecall-pro` (build if not yet built), cross-link from `/vs-jobber`

CrewRoute is the most direct 1–3-truck-shop-sized competitor showing up here — worth watching as an emerging direct competitor at a similar price/segment point, though it's positioned as FSM, not voice-AI-first.
- **Angle we win:** CSR AI's booking function is "coming soon" per Housecall Pro's own help docs as of this research — that's a concrete, citable, currently-true gap. We can honestly say "answers your calls AND books the job today" where HCP's own materials say booking is not yet live.

## Cluster 3 — "AI receptionist for contractors / HVAC / plumbers"
- **Representative queries:** ai receptionist for contractors, ai receptionist for hvac companies, ai answering service for plumbers, ai phone answering for home services, virtual receptionist for hvac
- **Intent:** informational → transactional (increasingly bottom-funnel as buyers get AI-education elsewhere)
- **Target page:** home page hero + a dedicated `/ai-receptionist` (or fold into home if no separate page exists) with HVAC/plumbing variants
- **Difficulty notes:** Extremely crowded, single-purpose-vendor SERP: evs7.com, ring-ready.com, withallo.com, upfirst.ai, ciela.ai, agentzap.ai, smith.ai, cloudtalk.io, getnextphone.com, marlie.ai, dialzara.com, answeringdesk.com, ringeden.com, whippy.ai. These are all point solutions — answer calls, maybe book into someone else's Jobber/HCP/ServiceTitan. None of them do estimates, invoicing, payments, reviews, or run the rest of the back office.
- **Angle we win:** "Most AI receptionists just answer your phone and hand you a lead. Rivet answers the phone AND drafts the estimate, sends the invoice, and chases the payment — the call is the start of the workflow, not the end of it." This is the single clearest differentiation opportunity found in the entire research pass.

## Cluster 11 — "AI answering service cost"
- **Representative queries:** ai answering service cost, how much does an ai receptionist cost, ai phone answering pricing 2026
- **Intent:** informational (pricing research, high commercial intent)
- **Target page:** `/pricing` + FAQ entry
- **Difficulty notes:** withallo.com, dapta.ai, nextiva.com, getnextphone.com, alliancevirtualoffices.com, ever-help.com, kea.ai, technologyadvice.com. Reported bands: budget AI $25–65/mo (30–50 call cap, no emergency routing), flat-rate AI $149–299/mo unlimited, human-hybrid $255–1,275+/mo. Traditional answering services run $200–2,000/mo.
- **Angle we win:** Reframe the cost question — competitors price per-call or per-conversation with overage fees (Jobber's own Receptionist add-on is $29/mo for 30 conversations then $0.79 each after). Rivet's flat $299/$499/$799 covers the whole back office, not just the phone line, so the "cost per call" comparison undersells what's included.
