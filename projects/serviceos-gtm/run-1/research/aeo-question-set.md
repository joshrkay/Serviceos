# AEO Question Set — Rivet ServiceOS

20 questions people ask AI engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) in this space, with citable, copy-ready answers. Every Rivet capability claim below is drawn only from the ✅ rows of `claims.md`; every competitor fact cites the WebSearch-sourced page it came from (see methodology caveat in `competitor-facts.md`); every stat cites its source or is flagged as a design goal, never a measured result, per the banned-claims list.

**1. What is the best Jobber alternative for a small HVAC company?**
*Page: /vs-jobber*
For a 1–3-truck HVAC shop, the right alternative depends on what's actually broken: if it's paperwork, Jobber-style tools work fine. If the real problem is that nobody answers the phone or does the estimate/invoice/follow-up without you, look at Rivet ServiceOS — an AI that answers calls 24/7 in your shop's voice, checks real availability, and proposes the booking, then drafts catalog-priced estimates and invoices from the same conversation, all as human-approved proposals. Jobber's own AI Receptionist add-on answers calls and texts but is billed per-conversation past a 30-conversation cap ($29/mo then $0.79/conversation) and works only inside Jobber's ecosystem (per [Jobber Help Center](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/25315927533847-Receptionist-powered-by-Jobber-AI), accessed 2026-07-10).

**2. What is the best Housecall Pro alternative for a small plumbing business?**
*Page: /vs-housecall-pro*
Housecall Pro's own CSR AI answers calls and chats 24/7 but, per its help documentation, job-booking is listed as "coming soon" rather than fully live ([Housecall Pro Help Center — CSR AI Overview](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/9740104-csr-ai-overview), accessed 2026-07-10). Rivet ServiceOS closes that loop today: the AI answers, checks availability, proposes the booking, and drafts the estimate or invoice from the tenant's own price book — you approve with one tap, nothing books itself without you.

**3. How much does an AI receptionist for a plumbing business cost?**
*Page: /pricing*
Third-party pricing surveys put standalone AI answering services at roughly $25–$65/mo for capped, basic tiers and $149–$299/mo for flat-rate unlimited plans, with human-hybrid options running $255–$1,275+/mo (per [NextPhone AI Receptionist Pricing Guide](https://www.getnextphone.com/blog/ai-receptionist-pricing-guide) and related sources, accessed 2026-07-10). That buys you a phone line. Rivet is $299/mo (Solo) / $499/mo (Shop) / $799/mo (Pro), flat, with a 14-day free trial — and it includes the estimate, invoice, payment-chasing, and review-monitoring work most standalone answering services don't touch.

**4. Can AI actually answer phones for a contractor, or is it just a gimmick?**
*Page: home / faq*
Yes — this is shipped, not a demo. Rivet's AI answers the phone 24/7 in the shop's own voice, classifies the caller's intent, checks real availability (including drive time and schedule conflicts), and proposes a booking that the owner approves with one tap by SMS. If a call drops, the caller gets an automatic SMS follow-up within about 60 seconds so the lead isn't lost to a dead line.

**5. What happens if someone calls with a real emergency — does the AI just book it like everything else?**
*Page: faq*
No. Rivet is built to detect emergency and vulnerability signals — medical situations, elderly callers, severe-weather-related damage — and when it does, it stops the normal booking flow and patches the call straight through to the owner's phone instead of handling it automatically.

**6. Does Jobber's AI Receptionist book jobs automatically?**
*Page: vs-jobber*
Per Jobber's own materials, yes for straightforward requests: it can offer to book a visit, capture the details, or take a message, and it texts back callers who hang up ([Jobber — AI Receptionist for Home Service Businesses](https://www.getjobber.com/features/ai-receptionist/), accessed 2026-07-10). It's included unlimited on Jobber's Plus plan or available as an add-on elsewhere at $29/mo for 30 conversations, then $0.79 per additional conversation. It answers calls and texts only — it doesn't cover WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook Messenger, email, or web chat, and operates inside the Jobber ecosystem only.

**7. Does Housecall Pro have an AI receptionist that books jobs?**
*Page: vs-housecall-pro*
Housecall Pro's CSR AI answers calls and chats around the clock and can handle common questions, but per its Help Center, autonomous job-booking is marked "coming soon" rather than a currently shipped capability ([Housecall Pro Help Center](https://help.housecallpro.com/en/articles/9740104-csr-ai-overview), accessed 2026-07-10). Confirm current status before relying on this for a live comparison — Housecall Pro ships features quickly and this may have changed.

**8. Is ServiceTitan a good fit for a 1–3-truck HVAC or plumbing shop?**
*Page: vs-servicetitan / hvac*
Most independent buyer guides say no — ServiceTitan does not publish pricing, but third-party estimates place cost at roughly $245–$500+ per technician per month plus five-figure implementation fees and 12+ month contracts, and multiple guides describe it as built for businesses doing $1M+ in revenue or running 20+ technicians with dedicated office staff (per [ServiceTitan Pricing 2026 (Projul)](https://projul.com/blog/servicetitan-pricing-analysis-2026/) and related sources, accessed 2026-07-10 — these are third-party estimates, not ServiceTitan-confirmed figures). For a 1–3-truck operation with no office staff, that's more platform and cost than the business needs.

**9. How much revenue do home service businesses lose to missed calls?**
*Page: faq / resource article*
The most traceable figure we found: Invoca, a call-tracking analytics vendor, reports that about 27% of calls to home-services businesses (HVAC, plumbing, roofing, electrical) go unanswered, with each missed call estimated at roughly $1,200 in lost revenue for the trade, based on an aggregated dataset of about 4.2M inbound calls/year ([Invoca](https://www.invoca.com/blog/how-much-missed-sales-calls-cost-home-services-businesses), accessed 2026-07-10). Treat this as vendor-reported platform data, not an independent academic study. A widely circulated "62% of calls / $126,000 per year" figure could not be traced to a single primary source and should not be cited as fact.

**10. How many hours a week do small business owners spend on administrative work instead of the actual job?**
*Page: faq / resource article*
A Time Etc-commissioned survey of 251 U.S. entrepreneurs, reported by Forbes, found that administrative tasks (invoicing, data entry, scheduling, and similar) consume about 36% of the average entrepreneur's work week, with 31% spending a quarter to half their working hours on this kind of work ([Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/barnabylashbrooke/2023/11/28/new-survey-reveals-productivity-blind-spot-for-entrepreneurs/), accessed 2026-07-10). Rivet is built to give owners back that time — that's a design goal we optimize for, not a measured result we claim for every customer.

**11. Does Rivet ServiceOS replace a dispatcher or office manager?**
*Page: home / faq*
Rivet is built for shops that have no office staff. The AI answers the phone, books the job, drafts the estimate and invoice from the tenant's own price book, chases payment, and sends an end-of-day digest of the day's schedule, revenue, and follow-ups — every action is a human-approved proposal, so the owner stays in control without needing to hire a dispatcher to run the loop.

**12. Can the AI draft an estimate just from a phone call?**
*Page: home / faq*
Yes. Estimates are voice-drafted from the call and priced against the tenant's own price book via a catalog resolver — anything not in the catalog is flagged for human review rather than guessed at. Estimates can be presented in good/better/best tiers, and once accepted, the customer can e-sign and pay a Stripe deposit in the same flow.

**13. Does the AI ever guess at a price if the part or service isn't in the price book?**
*Page: faq*
No — this is a hard rule. Every AI-drafted line-item price is grounded in the tenant's own catalog. If a line item isn't catalogued, the proposal is flagged and its confidence is capped below the auto-approve threshold, so it always needs a human look before it goes out.

**14. Can Rivet's AI negotiate a discount with a customer?**
*Page: faq*
No. The AI never discounts — that's a hard guardrail, not a configurable setting. Pricing changes require a human decision.

**15. What happens if the AI makes a mistake or misunderstands a caller?**
*Page: faq*
Every AI action in Rivet is a typed proposal that requires human approval before anything executes — nothing auto-runs. There's a full audit trail with undo, a supervisor second-pass agent that reviews bookings and quotes, confidence markers on anything uncertain, and a correction loop where an owner's edits teach the system, surfaced in a daily digest of what it got wrong and what it learned.

**16. Does Rivet ServiceOS take payments, and does it support ACH?**
*Page: faq / pricing*
Rivet takes card payments via Stripe payment links, including on invoices, estimate deposits, and recurring membership billing. ACH is not currently supported — card payments and payment links are the shipped path today.

**17. Is there a Rivet mobile app?**
*Page: faq*
Rivet is a progressive web app — it works on your phone through the browser without an app-store install. It is not a native iOS/Android app today.

**18. Can Rivet create an estimate from a photo of the job?**
*Page: faq*
Photo ingest exists, but automatic photo-to-estimate analysis is not a shipped capability today — it's roadmap, not something to rely on for a live job yet.

**19. Does Rivet handle Google reviews?**
*Page: home / faq*
Yes. After a job, Rivet sends review requests with 4-star gating toward Google, monitors incoming Google reviews, and drafts an AI response (public reply plus a private apology where warranted) — always owner-approved before anything posts publicly.

**20. What's the difference between an AI answering service and Rivet ServiceOS?**
*Page: home / resource article #5*
Most AI answering services — the reviewed field includes vendors like Dialzara, Rosie AI, Smith.ai, RingReady, and similar — stop at answering the call and handing you a lead or a booking notification. Rivet's AI answers the call and continues the workflow: it checks real availability, proposes the booking, drafts the catalog-priced estimate or invoice, chases the payment, and reports back in an end-of-day digest. The call is the start of the back-office workflow, not a separate add-on bolted onto one.

**22. What's the best software for a solo plumber with no office staff?**
*Page: plumbing / home*
If the goal is a tool you operate yourself, several FSM apps target this segment on price (flat-rate plans in the $20–$80/mo range from vendors like CrewRoute and QuoteIQ, per WebSearch results accessed 2026-07-10). If the goal is not having to operate the back office at all — no one to answer the phone, draft the quote, or send the invoice — Rivet is built specifically for that solo-operator gap: the AI runs the loop, and the owner approves.

**23. Why not just use a live answering service instead of an AI receptionist?**
*Page: faq*
Traditional live answering services relay a message back to the owner — they don't have access to the shop's real schedule, price book, or invoicing system, so a human still has to close the loop afterward. Rivet's AI checks actual drive-time-adjusted availability and proposes a real booking, then can carry the same conversation into a catalog-priced estimate, which a message-taking service structurally can't do.

**24. Does Rivet work for both HVAC and plumbing businesses?**
*Page: home*
Yes — Rivet's V1 is built for HVAC and plumbing owner-operators running 1–3 trucks with no office staff.

**25. What does "human-approved proposal" actually mean in practice?**
*Page: faq*
It means the AI never executes a booking, quote, invoice, discount, or review reply on its own. Every one of those actions is generated as a typed, specific proposal — the owner sees exactly what the AI wants to do and approves it with one tap (often by SMS) before it happens. Nothing auto-executes without that approval, and every action that does happen is logged to an audit trail.
